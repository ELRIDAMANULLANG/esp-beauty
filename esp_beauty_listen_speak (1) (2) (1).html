ESP for Beauty Department: Listening and Speaking

Enhancing English Skills for Real-World Beauty Practice

By Elfrida Simanullang

Introduction

The Beauty Department at vocational high schools (SMK) equips students with practical skills and theoretical knowledge related to hair styling, facial care, makeup application, and overall personal grooming. These competencies are essential in preparing students for careers in salons, spas, or independent beauty service businesses.

In todayâ€™s globalized beauty industry, English proficiency plays an important role, especially in understanding product descriptions, following beauty tutorials, and interacting with international clients. Therefore, English for Specific Purposes (ESP) tailored to the beauty field is highly beneficial for vocational students.

This ESP material focuses on developing listening and speaking skills using relevant scenarios and vocabulary specific to the beauty industry. Students will practice understanding and delivering English communication within professional beauty contexts.

Beauty Department Core Topics

Within the Beauty Department, students learn a variety of essential competencies including:

  • Facial Treatments
  • Basic and Professional Makeup Techniques
  • Hair Care and Styling
  • Manicure and Pedicure

In each of these areas, the ability to understand and use English is necessary, particularly for reading product labels, understanding usage instructions, and explaining beauty procedures to clients.

Unit 1: Listening - Understanding Facial Treatment Conversations

Learning Objective: Students will be able to comprehend and respond to a facial treatment consultation between a client and a therapist.

Material:

Listen to the following audio of a facial treatment consultation at a beauty salon.

Task Instructions:

After listening to the audio, answer these questions based on the conversation:

  1. What treatment is the client asking for?
  2. What is the therapist's advice for skin care?
  3. How often should the client get the treatment?

Unit 2: Speaking - Explaining Makeup Application Steps

Learning Objective: Students will be able to explain the steps of makeup application clearly in English.

Material:

Study the general steps of applying makeup: skin preparation, foundation, eye makeup, and lip color. Learn how to describe each step using appropriate English expressions.

Task Instructions:

Work in pairs. One student plays the role of a beauty therapist, and the other acts as a client. The therapist must explain each makeup step clearly using the following structure:

  • Ask the client's preferences and skin type.
  • Describe how to prep the skin and apply primer/foundation.
  • Explain the eye makeup process (eyeshadow, eyeliner, mascara).
  • Suggest appropriate lip colors.
  • End with a polite concluding statement.

Conclusion

After completing this ESP unit, students are expected to gain confidence in using English in real beauty service contexts. With stronger listening and speaking skills, they will be more prepared to communicate effectively with clients, understand professional dialogues, and express their expertise in English.
